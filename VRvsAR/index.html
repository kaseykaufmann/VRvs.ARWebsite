What Is Virtual Reality?

Virtual reality is a "full dive" experience that replaces your surroundings with a virtual environment. Most VR gear is non-transparent headsets that use six-degrees-of-freedom (6DOF) motion tracking made possible by external sensors. These detect any movement in the body; however that mobility is limited by a few square meters of space, and does not counteract the possibilities of bumping into objects within your real surroundings. Some headsets only function on three-degrees-of-freedom where only the movement and direction of the head is considered.

Technology: gyroscopes, motion sensors, screens, lightweight processors

How Does Healthcare Evolve?

The use of VR technology allows the opportunity for medical students and professionals to practice their craft, more specifically within surgical and life-threatening situations that can not be recreated in practice for obvious reasons. Virtual headsets could also simulate a meeting room for both patients and medical interviews, for instances where individuals would not have access to see each other in person. Future applications for this field also include general examinations and therapy sessions at the comfort of one’s home.

Virtual Reality Marketing

A 360-degree virtual reproduction of any desired location is a great way of enticing curious travelers who may have doubts towards buying their tickets. Museums such as the Louvre already offer virtual tours which showcase some of their artworks and installations. With VR, online marketing companies could provide a better look within products viewed by customers, and it is notably useful for real-estate and tourist attractions that rely on visuals as well as their use of space.

Virtual Reality And Education

From schools, universities, to training stations, virtual reality has slowly mounted its way to becoming a notable part of education. As mentioned, students have the ability to immerse themselves in virtual locations and situations, that would otherwise be unattainable in real life. VR displays detailed and interactive simulations that challenges most 2-D illustrations, ranging from human anatomy, the locations of stars within a galaxy, and almost any kind of data that could be visualized in three dimensions.

What Is Augmented Reality?

Augmented reality adds to our surroundings rather than completely replacing it. Most AR applications can easily function on 6DOF by tracking the user’s phone location. AR headsets, which are transparent in comparison, use camera or laser sensors. AR could definitely enhance one’s experiences with more mobility but does not offer as much immersion as VR.

Technology: mobile device, motion sensors, lightweight processors

What Are The Applications In Gaming For Augmented Reality?

While also applicable to VR, augmented reality has made far better strides in gaming consoles and applications, with Pokemon Go being the prime example of its success and popularity. With mobile devices as its main platform, AR within gaming is extremely accessible and relies on the concept of having virtual objects or characters become a part of real-world surroundings, which in turn makes them believable and concrete within a screen.

Augmented Reality In Retail

The increasing popularity and reliance on online shopping has led developers to think of new and innovative ways to have their products be tested through the digital lens. AR’s distinctive quality of allowing real surroundings to be seen can be essential for furniture stores that can show how any product would look within a given room. The same concept goes for clothing which could be “worn” by users through the touch of a button.

How AR Will Help In Navigation And Utilities

Among one of the developing fields in augmented reality is navigation. The brand Google Glass has spurred interest in wearable AR, with the promise of allowing users to move around and see the real world while witnessing icons that could help them in navigating direction or even searching information without having to look down on their phone. The utility of this kind of AR is comparable to smart watches that eliminate the use of a mobile device for quick and simple actions.

Similarities Between Virtual And Augmented Reality

Technology for virtual and augmented reality share the same fundamental mechanics when it comes to track the movements of any given device as well as the programming that goes into the tracking and development of realistic virtual objects within a digital screen.

Applications also coincide within augmented and virtual reality as companies take advantage of demand, modifying the any service based on the limitations and positive characteristics of either side to produce a similar output for each given experience. Although one field may work better with AR or vice versa, applications in communication, entertainment, and marketing are all obtainable in both technologies.

Both have applications to science and medicine. Notwithstanding some variations, both AR and VR have also received similar ratings and criticism when it comes to their use within the entertainment world. These technologies are receive repeated approvals when it comes to being the main platform for various games and media that would otherwise not have the same accolade without the immersive experience that augmented and virtual reality provide. Critics, on the other hand, argue that prolonged used of these products leads to added issues regarding eyesight deterioration and video game addiction.

Differences Between Virtual And Augmented Reality

Relatively speaking, VR is utilized to a higher extent in both standalone and PC-connected headsets.

PC headsets can provide the highest quality in both digital images and interaction as they are linked to a much faster processor. The drawback to these products is the lack of mobility and maneuverability within simulations, as well as the added risk of tangling or disconnecting the wires that connect these headsets to immobile machines.These devices are best suited for programs that simulate user within a sitting-down position or in cases where minimal movement is required. Great examples of PC headsets are the HTC Vive, Oculus Rift, and PlayStation VR.

Standalone headsets, on the other hand, provide much more flexibility in movement as they carry small and lightweight processors integrated within the devices. These headsets are more accessible to the public and can be connected to smartphones and small devices, however it is important to always take note of one’s surrounding as these headsets may or may not provide any indications of bumping into physical obstacles in the real surroundings. Products like the Samsung Gear VR are cheap, readily available, and can even be bundled with purchases of certain mobile devices.
